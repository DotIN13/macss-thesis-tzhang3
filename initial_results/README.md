# Tracing Ideology in Large Language Models: Proposal

## Description

This project investigates how large language models (LLMs) represent and express political ideology in the context of U.S. politics. The study compares LLM-generated responses to public opinion data and evaluates the effects of prompt framing on ideological expression.

We evaluate alignment with real-world ideological distributions using:

- Direct simulation of political identities via prompts
- Retrieval-augmented prompting from an ideologically-labeled news corpus
- Task-framed prompting pipelines to investigate model inconsistency

This repository includes code for data preparation, model prompting, analysis, and figure generation, as well as the LaTeX source of the research paper.

## Initial Results

### Figure: Ideological Trends in ANES 2024

<img src="src/figs/policy_alignment_bubbleplot.png" alt="Policy Attitudes by Ideological Self-Placement" width="700"/>

This bubble plot illustrates the alignment between self-reported ideology and policy attitudes in the ANES 2024 dataset. Liberal and conservative respondents display consistent, divergent trends on domestic issues like abortion and immigration, but more heterogeneity on foreign policy issues.

### Figure: Ideological Trends Extracted from Model

<img src="src/figs/policy_alignment_bubbleplot_custom_order.png" alt="Policy Attitudes by Ideological Self-Placement" width="700"/>

When directly prompted to answer survey questions as typical liberal/conservative individuals, the model generated outputs matching ANES distribution on domestic policy issues, but overly consistent on foreign policy domains.

### Table: Contradictory Right-Wing Perspectives Generated by Model on Ukraine Aid

| **Pro-Military Support / Hawkish Stance** | **Anti-Military Support / Dovish Stance** |
|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Right-wing politicians call for **stronger military support** and **sanctions against Russia**. | Right-wing politicians, particularly Trump supporters, may call for a **withdrawal of U.S. military support for Ukraine** and advocate for a more **pragmatic approach to Russian territorial claims**. |
| Right-wing politicians advocate for **tougher sanctions** and **military actions against Russia**. | Right-wing politicians emphasize **direct negotiations with Russia** and maintaining strong bilateral relations, prioritizing U.S.-Russia ties over Ukrainian involvement. |
| Right-wing politicians **support Ukraine**, focusing on **military strength**. | Right-wing politicians might be **skeptical of extensive involvement in Ukraine** or prefer to **focus on domestic issues**. |
| Right-wing politicians may **support strong actions against Russia**, even controversial ones. | Right-wing politicians **question Ukraine's accession to NATO** and **support direct negotiations with Russia**. |

These outputs illustrate the **striking variation within LLM-simulated conservative perspectives** generated under bias detection task. A plausible hypothesis would be that RLHF or training data prior leads to stereotyped conceptualization of ideology on the surface level, but task framing can elicit deeper, more diverse outputs.

## Reproducibility

To reproduce the main results and figures:

1. Load and run `src/anes.ipynb` to prepare and visualize ideological trends in ANES 2024 data.
2. Run `src/ideology.ipynb` to simulate LLM responses across prompt types and compare to survey baselines.
3. Figures will be saved to `src/figs/`.
